Air Quality Index (AQI) Basics

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a standardized metric used to communicate the quality of the air in a specific location. It provides a numerical value that corresponds to the overall air quality and indicates the potential health effects of breathing the air.

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is segmented into six groups, each indicating a distinct level of health risk. Additionally, each group is associated with a particular color, simplifying the process for individuals to promptly assess if air quality is deteriorating to unhealthy levels in their vicinity.


AQI Basics for Ozone and Particle Pollution

Daily AQI Colour Levels of Concern Values of Index Description of Air Quality
Green Good 0 to 50 Air quality is satisfactory,and air pollution poses little or no risk.
Yellow Moderate 51 to 100 Air quality is acceptable. However, there may be a risk for some people, particularly those who are unusually sensitive to air pollution.
Orange Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups 101 to 150 Members of sensitive groups may experience health effects. The general public is less likely to be affected.
Red Unhealthy 151 to 200 Some members of the general public may experience health effects; members of sensitive groups may experience more serious health effects.
Purple Very Unhealthy 201 to 300 Health alert: The risk of health effects is increased for everyone.
Maroon Hazardous 301 and higher Health warning of emergency conditions: everyone is more likely to be affected.

2023 IQAir World Air Quality

According to the 2023 IQAir World Air Quality Report, Southeast Asia faced increasing challenges with air quality, with climate conditions and transboundary haze being major contributing factors. PM2.5 concentrations rose in nearly every country in the region, highlighting the urgent need for air quality management measures. Additionally, the report identified Central & South Asia as home to the top ten most polluted cities in the world, with Begusarai, India, being the most polluted metropolitan area of 2023. India, in particular, was highlighted as home to the four most polluted cities globally. These findings underscore the pressing need for coordinated efforts to address air pollution and its impacts on public health and the environment in these regions.
