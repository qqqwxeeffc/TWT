Southeast Asia Air Quality

Air Pollution in Southeast Asia got worse in 2023

Only three out of 357 Southeast Asian cities met the World Health Organisation’s (WHO) PM2.5 public health guideline of no more than 5 micrograms of particulate pollution per cubic metre (µg/m3) in 2023 – down from eight regional cities in 2022.


In 2023, the Philippines was the only Southeast Asian nation to improve its air quality, with a 10% reduction in PM2.5 levels. Conversely, Indonesia emerged as the most polluted country in the region, experiencing a 20% increase in particulate pollution.

Seven cities in Indonesia, Vietnam, and Thailand exceeded WHO's annual PM2.5 guidelines by more than ten times, a significant rise from 2022 when no city surpassed an average of 50 µg/m³. Singapore was the least polluted Southeast Asian country, yet its PM2.5 levels were still more than four times above WHO's safe benchmark at 13.4 µg/m³.

Additionally, PM2.5 levels nearly tripled in Cambodia, increased by 30% in Malaysia, 28% in Thailand, and 9% in Vietnam. In response to chronic air pollution, ASEAN established a special unit to address the issue, while Greenpeace urged regional governments to enact laws to combat transboundary pollution and hold accountable companies contributing to cross-border smog.
